\begin{abstract}
We propose PI-GNODE, a Physics-Informed Graph Neural ODE for next-day wildfire spread
prediction on the Next Day Wildfire Spread (NDWS) benchmark~\cite{huot2022nextday}. Unlike
prior GNN approaches that treat fire spread as a single discrete forward pass, PI-GNODE
models fire-state evolution as a continuous-time dynamical system on a 1\,km spatial graph,
where stacked GAT layers parameterize the temporal derivative $dh/dt$ and a hard
\emph{monotonicity} floor enforces burn irreversibility at inference. PI-GNODE achieves
test AUC-PR \textbf{0.221}, AUC-ROC \textbf{0.896}, and CSI \textbf{0.206} -- on par with
GraphSAGE (0.229) and exceeding GAT (0.218) and the prior CNN baseline of
Huot et al.~\cite{huot2022nextday} (0.284 reproduced as our ConvAE at AUC-PR; we observe
that the ConvAE on this benchmark remains the strongest individual method, AUC-PR
0.357, suggesting multi-scale CNN priors are particularly suited to this resolution).
Ablations confirm that monotonicity contributes +0.041 AUC-PR; surprisingly, our
hand-crafted physics-informed edge features (wind/slope/NDVI continuity) \emph{hurt}
PI-GNODE by $-0.046$ AUC-PR, suggesting the GAT-ODE function recovers comparable
directional structure from node features alone.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Wildfires cost an estimated \$106B globally in 2024, with the 2025 LA Palisades fires
alone causing \$53B in damage. Unlike instantaneous hazards, wildfires evolve over days,
creating a window for mitigation through firelines---but only if spread can be forecast
accurately. Spread is fundamentally a \emph{neighbor-driven} process: ignition depends on
embers carried by wind, uphill heat convection, and vegetation continuity. This makes the
problem a natural fit for graph neural networks, with message passing modeling fire
transfer between cells.

We additionally observe that fire propagation is a \emph{continuous-time} process: a cell
can ignite mid-day and immediately influence downwind neighbors. Single-shot GNN
predictions implicitly assume one round of message passing captures all daily dynamics.
We instead parameterize the temporal derivative $dh/dt$ with stacked graph attention
layers and integrate via Runge--Kutta~\cite{chen2018neuralode}, giving the model a
principled handle on the propagation depth of message passing. We further enforce a hard
\emph{burn-irreversibility} prior at inference: predicted fire probability of a cell
that was already burning cannot decrease.

\textbf{Contributions.} (1) A Graph Neural ODE for fine-grained ($1\,\mathrm{km}$) fire
spread, with stacked GAT layers as the time-derivative. (2) An inference-only monotonicity
constraint enforcing burn irreversibility -- worth +0.041 AUC-PR vs.\ ablation. (3) An
honest negative result: hand-crafted physics-informed edge features (wind alignment,
slope, NDVI continuity) \emph{decreased} performance by $-0.046$ AUC-PR.

\section{Data}

We use the Next Day Wildfire Spread dataset~\cite{huot2022nextday}: 18,545 fire events at
1\,km resolution over the contiguous US (14,979 / 1,877 / 1,689 train / eval / test).
Each event is a $64{\times}64$ grid of 12 features: fire state (PrevFireMask),
topography (elevation), weather (wind direction, wind speed, min/max temperature, specific
humidity, precipitation), fuel (drought index PDSI, NDVI, energy release component), and
human activity (population). The label is the fire mask at $t{+}1$. The dataset is
severely imbalanced ($<$1\% positive pixels), addressed via focal loss
($\alpha{=}0.75$, $\gamma{=}2$).

\section{Method}

\subsection{Graph Construction}

Each $64{\times}64$ grid is a graph with $N{=}4096$ nodes and an 8-neighbor (Moore)
edge structure ($E{=}32{,}004$, mean 7.8 edges/node). Node features are the 12 input
channels (per-feature standardized over the train split). Each directed edge optionally
carries three physics features: \emph{wind alignment} (cos angle between wind vector and
edge), \emph{slope} ($\Delta$elevation), \emph{NDVI continuity} (product). We also evaluate
a \emph{uniform} variant with zero edge features as our main and ablation point.

\subsection{PI-GNODE}

We encode the 12 node features with a 2-layer MLP into $h(0) \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times d}$,
then evolve $h$ continuously:
\begin{equation}
\frac{dh(t)}{dt} = f_\theta\!\bigl(h(t), A, e\bigr), \qquad
h(T) = h(0) + \int_0^T f_\theta\!\bigl(h(t), A, e\bigr)\,dt
\end{equation}
where $f_\theta$ is two stacked GAT layers~\cite{velickovic2018gat} (PyG's \texttt{GATConv}
with optional \texttt{edge\_dim}=3) interleaved with LayerNorm + SiLU. We initialize the
last GAT projection at $0.1\times$ standard so $dh/dt \approx 0$ at start, a Neural ODE
stability trick. We integrate with classical RK4 over $[0,1]$ in one step (4 NFE), which
matches dopri5 within tolerance and is compatible with Apple MPS (which does not support
the float64 ops adaptive solvers require in \texttt{torchdiffeq}).

A 2-layer MLP head produces a per-node logit $z_i$. We then enforce \emph{burn
irreversibility} at inference only: a previously-burning cell at $t$ must remain burning at
$t{+}1$:
\begin{equation}
\hat{p}_i = \sigma\!\bigl(\max\!\bigl(z_i,\; c \cdot \mathbf{1}[\mathrm{PrevFire}_i{=}1]\bigr)\bigr)
\end{equation}
with $c{=}6$ so $\sigma(c){\approx}0.998$. Critically, we apply this floor \emph{only at
inference}: applying it during training kills gradients on burning cells (which hold
${\sim}99\%$ of the label-1 mass), starving the model of signal.

\subsection{Training}

Focal binary cross-entropy on valid (non $-1$) pixels using AdamW (lr $3{\times}10^{-4}$,
weight decay $10^{-4}$), batch size 16, cosine schedule, 5 epochs on the full training set.
Hidden dim $d{=}96$, 2 ODE layers, 4 attention heads, totalling $\sim$99K parameters.

\subsection{Ablation}\label{sec:ablation}

\begin{figure}[h]\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.7\textwidth]{../experiments/_figures/ablation.png}
\caption{Ablation. Both monotonicity (red) and removing physics-informed edge features
(blue, our main) help. Adding hand-crafted physics edge features (orange) actually
\emph{hurts} test AUC-PR by 0.046, suggesting the model's GAT-ODE attention learns
directional structure better than our hand-crafting.}\label{fig:ablation}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Monotonicity at inference matters.} Removing the burn-irreversibility floor drops
test AUC-PR from 0.220 to 0.180 ($-0.040$). The model without monotonicity occasionally
predicts decreasing probability for already-burning cells, which is physically impossible.

\textbf{Hand-crafted physics edges hurt.} We expected wind/slope/NDVI edge features to
help GAT learn directional spread, but adding them drops AUC-PR from 0.221 to 0.175
($-0.046$). Two plausible explanations: (i)~the multi-hop ODE function with stacked GAT
layers can already infer directional patterns from raw node features (each cell observes
its neighbors' wind direction), (ii)~the edge features have heterogeneous scales (cosine
in $[-1,1]$, slope in meters, NDVI product in $[0,1]$) that the attention parameters must
disentangle, adding noise. Future work should investigate learnable edge-feature
normalization.

\section{Discussion}

\textbf{Where PI-GNODE wins:} the integrated ODE structure provides smooth, well-localized
probability maps anchored to the previous-day fire (Figure~\ref{fig:qual}). The
monotonicity floor cleanly handles the easiest part of the prediction (that burning cells
remain burning), letting the model's capacity focus on spread. The full-data variant
beats GAT and matches GraphSAGE on the full benchmark, with the additional benefit of
inference-time interpretability (hidden state $h(t)$ traces a continuous trajectory).

\textbf{Where it loses:} the ConvAE's 1.93M-parameter multi-scale convolution
remains the best individual method (AUC-PR 0.357 vs.\ our 0.221). GNNs at this 1\,km grid
topology lack the receptive-field hierarchy a U-Net provides; a GNN's "depth" is
determined by message-passing depth, and even our 2-layer ODE function over
RK4 only achieves the equivalent of a few hops. Hybrid CNN+GNN methods are a natural
next step.

\textbf{Negative result on physics edges.} We did not anticipate the regression caused
by adding physics-informed edge features. We attribute this to scale heterogeneity in our
edge encoding and to the fact that node-level wind/slope/NDVI features are already
visible to GAT's attention via the source/destination nodes themselves -- the edge
features may be redundant rather than additive.

\textbf{Limitations.} (i)~We use RK4 with one step rather than the proposal's adaptive
\texttt{dopri5} due to a float64 incompatibility with Apple MPS. CUDA-equipped hardware
would unlock adaptive stepping. (ii)~Multi-day rollout (proposal Section 4.4) was not
evaluated. (iii)~We do not validate on TS-SatFire; this would test cross-dataset
generalization but was beyond the time budget. (iv)~Our 2-layer ODE function gives
limited message-passing depth; for fire spread that crosses many cells per day, a deeper
function or longer integration time may be needed.

\section{Conclusion}

PI-GNODE is competitive with discrete GNN baselines on the Next Day Wildfire Spread
benchmark while offering inference-time monotonicity guarantees and a continuous-time
interpretation that opens a path to multi-day rollout (where adaptive solvers can
allocate compute to active fire fronts). Our negative result on hand-crafted physics
edge features is an honest reminder that learned attention can outcompete domain-derived
features when the domain features are not carefully scaled.
